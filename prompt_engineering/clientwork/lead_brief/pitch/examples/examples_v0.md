# Example Pitches — 4 Versions for Discussion

These demonstrate the system across different lead profiles and card matches. Same prompt, different inputs, each producing a targeted pitch.

---

## Example 1: Construction Firm → Business Gold

### Input

```
### LEAD DATA
- Company: Apex Construction LLC
- Industry: Construction
- Annual Revenue: $6,000,000
- Employees: 8
- Annual Spend: $960,000
- Monthly Expenses: $80,000
- Top Spend Categories: Construction materials ($300K/yr), Equipment rentals ($180K/yr), Gas/fuel ($60K/yr), Advertising ($48K/yr)
- Current Payment: Checks 45%, Debit 35%, Credit card 20%
- Cards Held: Chase Ink Business Preferred
- Travel Spend: $15,000
- Large Purchases (≥$5K/yr): 18

### DERIVED SIGNALS
- Program Type: Business
- Recommended Card: Business Gold
- Travel Intensity: Low
- Category Concentration: Concentrated
- Non-Card Payment %: 80%
- Card Gap: New (no AMEX)
```

### Generated Pitch

> Apex Construction is a $6M construction firm running roughly $80K/month in operating expenses — with spend heavily concentrated in materials, equipment, gas, and advertising. Right now, 80% of that spend is going through checks and debit, which means they're leaving significant rewards on the table. The **Business Gold Card** is a natural fit here — it automatically earns 4x points on their top 2 spending categories each billing cycle, and with $60K in annual gas spend and $48K in advertising, those two categories alone could generate over 400,000 Membership Rewards points per year. Shifting even a portion of their check and debit payments to the Gold Card turns existing expenses into tangible returns without changing what they buy. *"I noticed your business does a lot of work across materials and fuel — I'd love to share how some construction firms are turning that spend into real value."*

**Why this works**: Industry-specific, cites actual spend numbers from the data, maps two specific categories to the Gold Card's 4x auto-select mechanic, quantifies the rewards, and addresses the check/debit gap.

## Example 2: Marketing Agency → Business Gold (Travel Secondary)

### Input

```
### LEAD DATA
- Company: Brightwave Digital
- Industry: Marketing & Advertising
- Annual Revenue: $4,200,000
- Employees: 12
- Annual Spend: $720,000
- Monthly Expenses: $60,000
- Top Spend Categories: Digital advertising/media buying ($240K/yr), Software/SaaS subscriptions ($96K/yr), Restaurants/client meals ($36K/yr), Wireless/telecom ($18K/yr)
- Current Payment: Credit card 50%, ACH 30%, Checks 20%
- Cards Held: Capital One Spark Cash
- Travel Spend: $42,000
- Large Purchases (≥$5K/yr): 8

### DERIVED SIGNALS
- Program Type: Business
- Recommended Card: Business Gold
- Travel Intensity: Moderate
- Category Concentration: Concentrated
- Non-Card Payment %: 50%
- Card Gap: New (no AMEX)
```

### Generated Pitch

> Brightwave Digital is a $4.2M marketing agency spending $60K/month — with a heavy concentration in digital advertising, software, client meals, and telecom. Those four categories happen to be exact matches for the **Business Gold Card's** 4x rewards categories, which auto-selects the top 2 each billing cycle. With $240K/yr in media buying and $96K in software alone, they'd earn 4x Membership Rewards on whichever two are highest each month — far outperforming the flat cashback from their current Capital One Spark. On top of that, their $42K in travel spend would earn 3x on flights and hotels booked through Amex Travel. *"I saw that you're in the digital marketing space — a lot of agencies don't realize how well the Gold Card's category structure maps to their spend mix. Mind if I walk you through it?"*

**Why this works**: Highlights the 4-of-6 category alignment (advertising, software, restaurants, wireless — all hit), directly addresses competitive displacement from Capital One Spark, and layers on the travel secondary benefit.

## Example 3: Engineering Firm → Corporate Platinum + Purchase Card

### Input

```
### LEAD DATA
- Company: Sterling Engineering Group
- Industry: Engineering Services
- Annual Revenue: $18,000,000
- Employees: 85
- Annual Spend: $3,200,000
- Monthly Expenses: $267,000
- Top Spend Categories: Travel/flights ($480K/yr), Hotels ($240K/yr), Equipment/supplies ($360K/yr), Software licenses ($180K/yr), Procurement/office ($120K/yr)
- Current Payment: Corporate Visa 60%, ACH 25%, Checks 15%
- Cards Held: Visa Corporate Card
- Travel Spend: $720,000
- Large Purchases (≥$5K/yr): 36

### DERIVED SIGNALS
- Program Type: Corporate
- Recommended Card: Corporate Platinum
- Travel Intensity: High
- Category Concentration: Dispersed
- Non-Card Payment %: 40%
- Card Gap: New (competitive displacement from Visa)
```

### Generated Pitch

> Sterling Engineering is an $18M firm with 85 employees and $3.2M in annual business spend — with $720K going to travel alone across flights, hotels, and field assignments. They're currently on a Visa corporate card for 60% of spend, with the remaining 40% still flowing through ACH and checks. The **Corporate Platinum Card** would give their executives premium lounge access and airline credits on that heavy travel spend, plus 5% cashback on employee cards — across 85 employees, that adds up fast. For their equipment and office procurement ($480K+/yr), the **Corporate Purchase Card** at $0 annual fee gives field teams a controlled purchasing tool without adding card fees. Also worth exploring: shifting ACH/check payments onto the card program to consolidate spend visibility and capture rewards on the full $3.2M. *"I was looking at your team's travel footprint — it's substantial. Are your folks getting lounge access and airline credits today, or is that something that would be valuable?"*

**Why this works**: Corporate multi-card recommendation (Platinum + Purchase Card), directly addresses the Visa displacement opportunity, uses the 85-employee scale to justify the 5% cashback multiplier effect, and the opener targets a tangible perk (lounge access) executives care about.

## Example 4: Sparse Data — Retail Business → Business Green (Safe Default)

### Input

```
### LEAD DATA
- Company: Main Street Boutique
- Industry: Retail
- Annual Revenue: $4,100,000
- Employees: 6
- Annual Spend: Not available
- Monthly Expenses: Not available
- Top Spend Categories: Not available
- Current Payment: Not available
- Cards Held: Not available
- Travel Spend: Not available
- Large Purchases (≥$5K/yr): Not available

### DERIVED SIGNALS
- Program Type: Business
- Recommended Card: Business Green
- Travel Intensity: Unknown
- Category Concentration: Unknown
- Non-Card Payment %: Unknown
- Card Gap: Unknown
```

### Generated Pitch

> Main Street Boutique is a $4.1M retail business with a small team of 6. While we don't yet have visibility into their full expense profile, retail businesses at this scale typically manage significant spend across inventory, shipping, marketing, and daily operations — all categories where Membership Rewards points accumulate quickly. The **Business Green Card** at $95/year is a strong starting point — it earns rewards across all general business purchases and gives a small team straightforward expense management without the complexity of category tracking. As we learn more about their specific spend patterns during the conversation, we can assess whether the **Business Gold Card's** 4x category structure would yield even more value. *"I'd love to understand more about how you manage purchasing for the store — are you all handling expenses through one account, or is it spread across a few different methods?"*

**Why this works**: Doesn't hallucinate missing data. Uses industry-level knowledge (retail = inventory, shipping, marketing) to fill contextual gaps. Recommends a safe default (Green) while teeing up Gold as an upgrade path. The opener is designed to discover the missing spend data during the call.
