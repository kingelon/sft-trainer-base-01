## With Existing AMEX Relationship

### Example A — Business Gold Recommendation
> Lead: ~$4M restaurant, AO has Hilton Honors Aspire card, ~$33K yearly spend on merchandise & supplies, top category also includes restaurants.

**Pitch:**
Andy Miller's restaurant does about $4M in revenue. He currently spends ~$33K a year on merchandise and supplies through a Hilton Honors Aspire card, which earns Hilton points — useful for hotel stays, but not rewarding the steady operational spend a restaurant racks up every month. The Business Gold Card earns 4x Membership Rewards on restaurants and automatically selects his top 2 spending categories each billing cycle. Andy keeps his Aspire for personal travel, and puts business spend on a card that actually rewards it.

### Example B — Business Platinum Recommendation
> Same lead, different card rec based on signals (large purchases, some travel).

**Pitch:**
Andy Miller runs a $4M restaurant and puts about $33K a year in supplies through his Hilton Honors Aspire — a consumer travel card that earns Hilton points, not business rewards. The Business Platinum Card earns 2x Membership Rewards on every purchase over $5K and 1x on everything else, plus gives Andy lounge access and airline credits he'd actually use. His supply spend starts earning MR points instead of hotel-only points, and any bigger equipment purchases automatically hit the 2x tier. He's already in the AMEX ecosystem — this just moves business spend onto a card built for it.

### Example C — Corporate Platinum Recommendation (if propensity signals corporate fit)
> Same lead, but propensity score suggests corporate card is the better path.

**Pitch:**
Andy Miller's restaurant does about $4M in revenue with ~$33K in yearly supply spend currently going through a Hilton Honors Aspire card. The Aspire earns Hilton points on personal spend, but doesn't offer company-level expense controls or Membership Rewards. A Corporate Platinum Card gives Andy company liability, centralized billing, and 5x on flights booked through AmexTravel — separating business expenses from personal cards while earning rewards that work across travel, transfers, and statement credits.

## Without AMEX Relationship

### Example D — Business Gold Recommendation (no existing cards)
> Lead: ~$4M business services firm, no AMEX relationship, no card detail available. Top spend likely in software and advertising.

**Pitch:**
Sarah Chen's consulting firm does $4.2M in revenue with no AMEX card in place. Her software subscriptions and digital advertising — both regular monthly expenses — fall directly into the Business Gold Card's 4x category list, which auto-selects her top 2 qualifying categories each billing cycle. At $375 a year, the Gold starts earning from month one on spend that's currently returning nothing.

### Example E — Corporate Platinum Recommendation (no existing cards, larger team)
> Lead: ~$5M company, 30 employees, no AMEX, likely distributed spend across team.

**Pitch:**
James Ortega's logistics company does $5M with 30 employees and no AMEX relationship. With a team that size, vendor payments and employee travel are probably flowing through personal cards or bank transfers with no consolidated view. The Corporate Platinum Card puts it under one program — company liability, centralized billing, 5x on flights via AmexTravel, and 5% cashback on employee card spend across all 30 team members.
